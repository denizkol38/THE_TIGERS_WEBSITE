About Us

A light pigmentation is caused by the cub inheriting the recessive gene from both sides. Pink nose, pink paws, grey and white mottled face, ice blue eyes, white to cream coat with black-grey-brown stripes. white tigers can mate with orange ones, but the offspring will be heterozygous for the recessive gene and will have orange fur... the only exception is if the mated orange tiger is also heterozygous, in which case there is a 50% chance that the offspring will be white with a double recessive gene. Compared to tigers with normal orange pigmentation, white tigers are larger than orange tigers both at birth and later in life.

There are White Tigers without stripes. Stage magicians Siegfried & Roy were the first to try to selectively breed tigers for stripelessness;[2] they had snow-white Bengal tigers from the Cincinnati Zoo (Tsumura, Mantra, Mirage and Akbar-Kabul) and Guadalajara, Mexico (Vishnu and Jahan), as well as a stripeless Siberian tiger named Apollo. These tigers are also called Snow Tigers in India. This is due to the low opacity of their stripes, which in the right light makes them appear to have no stripes.
